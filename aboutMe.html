Design Philosophy

Purpose is power — my prime directive.

In order to be successful design has to have a purpose; A reason for being; A method behind its madness. Without purpose the design process, and therefore the design itself, lacks direction and ends up without meaning.

Purpose in a design is the first step of the design process, even before any mood-boards or personas; It establishes a goal to aim towards, and therefore can help identify the tools and features needed to get there. For example, defining the purpose of a design as “To inform” will lead the design in a very different direction than if the purpose were “To persuade”. The design might feature more text or statistics versus emotional imagery.

Purpose also establishes a target audience; Knowing the purpose gives way to knowing what demographic my need a design with such purpose. With a target audience established the design can then be made with the obstacles they might face in mind, and open opportunities to involve some of the target audience in the design process, making the design that much more functional and useful.

Design without purpose is static, sterile, lacking both message and the tools needed to convey the message.
